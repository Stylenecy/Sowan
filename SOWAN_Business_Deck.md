## 3. PROBLEM STATEMENT

### 3.1 The Language-Culture Gap

**The Challenge:**
When learning a language, most people master vocabulary and grammar. However, when it comes to actual interaction, they still feel hesitant. The real challenge isn't the language itself — it's understanding the **culture and social context** behind it.

**The Pain Points:**
1. **Surface-Level Learning** — Apps teach words, not meanings
2. **Cultural Misunderstanding** — Same words have different implications in different contexts
3. **Lack of Confidence** — Even good students freeze in real conversations
4. **AI Fatigue** — Repetitive, soulless interactions that feel meaningless

### 3.2 The Elderly Isolation Problem

Indonesia has approximately **33.9 million elderly citizens** (ages 60+), representing about **12% of the population**. Many possess:

- **Decades of professional experience** — Careers spanning decades of real-world challenges
- **Deep cultural knowledge** — Traditional practices, stories, and wisdom passed through generations
- **Rich storytelling capabilities** — The ability to make learning come alive
- **Patient communication styles** — Unhurried, empathetic, understanding

**However:**
- Limited opportunities for economic participation after retirement
- Often feel isolated from modern society
- Skills and wisdom go underutilized
- Stereotyped as "unproductive" despite immense value

### 3.3 The Market Disconnect

| Global Need | Local Supply |
|-------------|--------------|
| Cultural integration support | 183,000+ foreign workers in Indonesia |
| Authentic cultural learning | Elderly wisdom largely untapped |
| Meaningful cross-generational connections | Limited platforms bridging this gap |

## 4. OUR SOLUTION: SOWAN.ID

### 4.1 What is SOWAN?

**SOWAN** (Indonesian word meaning "to visit" or "to seek wisdom from an elder") is an **edutech marketplace platform** that connects Indonesian youth and international learners with elderly mentors ("sesepuh") for meaningful 1-on-1 video call sessions.

**The SOWAN Difference:**

| Others Teach... | SOWAN Teaches... |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Language | **Communication** |
| Vocabulary | **Context** |
| Grammar Rules | **Cultural Wisdom** |
| Language Skills | **Life Experience** |
| Knowledge Transfer | **Storytelling** |

### 4.2 Why Elderly Mentors?

Elderly mentors provide something **technology cannot replicate:**

1. **Contextual Learning** — Experience-based explanations that make language come alive. When an elderly mentor explains "selamat pagi," they share the story behind the greeting, not just the words.

2. **Patient Guidance** — Unhurried, empathetic approach suited for anxious learners. No judgment, no rushing — just genuine conversation at the learner's pace.

3. **Authentic Storytelling** — Real life stories create lasting impressions. "Once, I used this phrase with a Dutch trader in 1972..." — stories that textbooks can't provide.

4. **Cultural Depth** — Generational wisdom passed through conversation. Why does batik have spiritual meaning? Why does "gotong royong" matter? The answers live in those who practiced it.

5. **Emotional Connection** — Human warmth that AI/computers cannot match. A genuine laugh, a knowing nod, a shared moment of understanding.

### 4.3 Vision & Mission

**Vision:**
> Supporting global cross-cultural integration while contributing to SDGs 4, 8, and 10.

**Mission:**
> Creating meaningful economic opportunities for elderly while providing authentic cultural education to global learners.

## 5. BUSINESS MODEL

### 5.1 Revenue Model

**Commission-Based (30% per session)**

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Platform Commission | 30% of session price |
| Mentor Earnings | 70% of session price |
| Session Pricing | Rp 66,000 — Rp 350,000 per hour |
| Target | 2,000 sessions per month by Year 2 |

### 5.2 Revenue Flow (Explicit)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    SOWAN PAYMENT FLOW                           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Step 1: LEARNER PAYS
         │
         │ Rp 100,000 (example session)
         ▼
┌─────────────────────┐
│   SOWAN PLATFORM    │  ← Receives full payment
│   (Payment Gateway) │
└─────────────────────┘
         │
         │ 30% Platform Commission = Rp 30,000
         ▼
┌─────────────────────┐
│   SOWAN TREASURY    │  ← Platform revenue
│   (Operations, R&D, │
│    Marketing)       │
└─────────────────────┘

Step 2: MENTOR RECEIVES
         │
         │ 70% Mentor Share = Rp 70,000
         ▼
┌─────────────────────┐
│   MENTOR WALLET     │  ← Direct to elderly mentor
│   (Weekly Payout)   │
└─────────────────────┘
```

**Why 70/30?**
- 70% to mentors ensures meaningful income for elderly
- 30% covers platform operations, development, and growth
- Aligns platform success with mentor satisfaction

### 5.3 Unit Economics

| Metric | Value | Explanation |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | Rp 15,000-25,000 | Digital marketing, content costs |
| **LTV (Lifetime Value)** | Rp 180,000+ | Average user does 3+ sessions |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | 7-12x | Healthy for subscription-light model |
| **Break-even Sessions** | ~500/month | To cover fixed costs |
| **Target Sessions** | 2,000/month | For profitability (Year 2) |

**Break-even Logic:**
```
Fixed Costs (Platform + Ops): ~Rp 30 million/month
Commission per session: Rp 30,000 (30% of Rp 100K avg)
Break-even: 30M ÷ 30K = 1,000 sessions/month
Target: 2,000 sessions/month = Rp 60M net margin
```

### 5.4 Cost Structure

**Fixed Costs:**
- Platform development & maintenance
- Server infrastructure

- Compliance & security

**Variable Costs:**
- Payment gateway fees (~2-3%)
- Marketing & acquisition costs
- Mentor training programs

### 5.5 Growth Model

Business growth aligns directly with:
- Increased session volume → Higher commission revenue
- Higher user retention → Improved LTV
- Mentor network expansion → More supply for demand
- B2B partnerships → Bulk bookings, stable revenue

## 7. MENTOR CURATION & QUALITY

### 7.1 Mentor Classification System

**Three Tiers:**

| Tier | Indonesian | Description | Price Range | Target Market |
|------|------------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Sahabat** | Friend | Entry-level, basic topics | Rp 66,000-90,000 | Casual learners |
| **Pemandu** | Guide | Intermediate, specialized knowledge | Rp 100,000-180,000 | Serious learners |
| **Maestro** | Master | Expert, deep expertise & cultural authority | Rp 200,000-350,000 | Premium/intensive |

### 7.2 Mentor Recruitment Process

**Step 1: Community Partnership**
- Partner with elderly community organizations
- Socialize program benefits and requirements
- Identify potential mentors through trusted channels

**Step 2: Initial Screening**
- Age verification (55+)
- Basic communication assessment
- Cultural knowledge evaluation
- Interest in teaching/sharing

**Step 3: Digital Readiness Training**
- **Geronteknologi Training Program:**
  - Basic smartphone/computer operation
  - Video call platform usage
  - Session scheduling tools
  - Simple, patient instruction
  - Ongoing technical support

**Step 4: Pedagogical Orientation**
- How to share stories effectively
- Active listening techniques
- Adapting to learner pace
- Creating engaging conversations

### 7.3 Quality Control Loop

**Continuous Quality Assurance:**

| Stage | Mechanism | Action if Issues Arise |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Pre-Session** | Mentor readiness checklist | Training refresh |
| **During Session** | User feedback buttons | Real-time monitoring |
| **Post-Session** | Rating & review system | Performance review |
| **Periodic** | Monthly quality assessment | Tier demotion or exit |

**Rating Thresholds:**
- ⭐ 4.5+ : Eligible for tier upgrade
- ⭐ 3.5-4.5: Standard monitoring
- ⭐ Below 3.5: Performance improvement plan
- ⭐ Below 3.0 (2+ consecutive months): Mentor exit process

## 10. RISK & MITIGATION

### 10.1 Identified Risks

| Risk Category | Specific Risk | Likelihood | Impact |
|---------------|---------------|------------|--------|
| **Market** | User rejection of elderly tutors | Medium | High |
| **Market** | Preference for younger tutors | Medium | Medium |
| **Operations** | Mentor quality inconsistency | High | High |
| **Operations** | Mentor drop-out/digital gap | Medium | Medium |
| **Technical** | Platform scalability issues | Low | High |
| **Competition** | Established platforms copying model | Low | Medium |
| **Regulatory** | Elderly employment regulations | Low | Medium |

### 10.2 Risk Mitigation Strategies

**Risk: User Rejection of Elderly Tutors**

*Mitigation:*
- Strong onboarding explaining unique value
- Free trial sessions to reduce perceived risk
- Showcase mentor stories and credentials prominently
- Gradual introduction: start with Sahabat tier for nervous users

**Risk: Preference for Younger Tutors**

*Mitigation:*
- Differentiate clearly: "We offer experience, not youth"
- Target users already frustrated with apps (our ideal customer)
- Highlight unique outcomes: confidence, cultural understanding
- Use testimonials from users who were skeptical then became advocates

**Risk: Mentor Quality Inconsistency**

*Mitigation:*
- Multi-stage curation process (screening → training → monitoring)
- Clear quality standards with tier demotion for poor performance
- Exit process for mentors below 3.0 rating
- Monthly mentor performance reviews
- User feedback loop embedded in platform

**Risk: Mentor Digital Gap**

*Mitigation:*
- Geronteknologi platform design (larger UI, simpler UX)
- Comprehensive digital training program
- Ongoing technical support hotline
- Family/community support encouraged during onboarding
- Device provisioning assistance if needed

**Risk: Scalability**

*Mitigation:*
- Cloud-native architecture for easy scaling
- Phased mentor recruitment (quality over quantity)
- Automated booking and payment systems
- Self-service mentor dashboard

### 10.3 Contingency Plans

| Scenario | Contingency |
|----------|-------------|
| Mentor shortage | Partner with more elderly organizations; accelerate recruitment |
| High churn | Implement mentor retention bonuses; improve working conditions |
| Low demand | Pivot B2B focus; develop corporate packages |
| Platform issues | Prioritize fixes; communication transparency with users |
